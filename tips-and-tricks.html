The start is arguably the most critical part of any sprint. A fraction of a second lost here is hard to recover.

  • Listen for the cue: Pay close attention to the starting signal (visual or audio).
  • Reaction Time: Practice reacting *immediately* to the signal. Don't anticipate, but be ready.
  • Initial Strides: Your first few inputs should be powerful and quick to build momentum rapidly.

Speed Stars is often about finding and maintaining the perfect rhythm with your controls, not just mashing buttons wildly.

  • Consistency is Key: Once you find a good rhythm, strive to maintain it throughout the race. Avoid erratic inputs.
  • Listen to Your Runner (Figuratively): Sometimes the game's animation or sound can give clues about optimal rhythm.

Like any skill, getting good at Speed Stars takes practice.

  • Focus on One Thing at a Time: Try to improve one aspect of your race (e.g., the start) before moving to the next.
